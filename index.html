The Climate Challenge

Egypt faces rising temperatures, increasing CO₂ emissions, and expanding arid regions due to overpopulation and urban congestion. In Siwa Oasis, temperatures can exceed 43 °C while rainfall drops to only 2 mm annually.

This creates soil desertification, reduced vegetation, and severe heat absorption. Mechanical cooling systems are often too expensive or impractical for remote villages such as Aghurmi.

Our Solution – BambuVent

Vision

BambuVent is a climate‑resilient building prototype designed to restore indoor comfort in extreme desert climates without electricity.

Material Philosophy

Bamboo, palm fronds, and rice straw are used as sustainable, low‑cost materials with high thermal mass and strong structural properties.

Why It Works

  • 75° inclined solar chimney for maximum heat absorption
  • Natural airflow driven by pressure and temperature differences
  • King post truss system for load distribution
  • Fire‑resistant and structurally stable under static and lateral loads

Engineering & Performance

The solar chimney heats internal air, reducing its density and creating buoyancy. This pulls cooler air from lower vents while expelling hot air from the top.

Calculations using stack pressure and Bernoulli’s equation showed a pressure difference of 0.00167 Pa and airflow velocity of 0.053 m/s, validating the passive cooling concept.

Stability analysis confirmed a safety factor of approximately 1.8, ensuring resistance against overturning.

Real‑Life Application

Aghurmi Village in Siwa Oasis is the recommended implementation site. Integrating the solar chimney into traditional Kashif stone and mud‑mortar buildings improves thermal comfort while preserving cultural identity.

Vegetative shading, proper orientation, and metal surfaces inside the chimney can further enhance performance.
